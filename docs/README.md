# OctoAcme Project Management Documentation

Welcome! This repository centralizes the OctoAcme team's core project management processes and living knowledge base. Use this README to quickly find process docs or share with new teammates for onboarding and ongoing reference.

## Project Management at OctoAcme — Summary

OctoAcme follows a structured, iterative approach to project management built on customer-first thinking and clear ownership. Every project moves through five lifecycle stages — **Initiation**, **Planning**, **Execution**, **Release**, and **Retrospective** — each supported by lightweight documentation, checklists, and defined roles. Key artifacts such as the Project One-pager, Risk Register, and Release Notes ensure that decisions and context are always captured and accessible to the whole team.

Roles are clearly defined across Developers, Product Managers, and Project Managers, each with specific responsibilities and communication modes. Developers focus on reliable delivery and quality, Product Managers own the backlog and outcomes, and Project Managers coordinate schedules, risks, and stakeholder communication. This shared understanding of ownership reduces ambiguity and accelerates cross-functional collaboration.

Risk management and communication are treated as continuous, proactive activities. Risks are identified early and tracked in a living Risk Register throughout the project lifecycle, while stakeholder updates follow a regular cadence (weekly syncs, monthly reports, ad-hoc escalations). A tiered escalation path — from team-level triage to sponsor-level decisions — ensures that blockers are resolved at the right level without unnecessary delays.

Continuous improvement is embedded in the culture through regular retrospectives after every sprint, release, or milestone. Teams capture what went well and what could be improved, convert insights into tracked action items, and measure the impact of changes over time. Combined with automated CI/CD, security scanning, and consistent PR conventions, OctoAcme's processes are designed to deliver reliably, learn continuously, and scale institutional knowledge across the organization.
